Show that the cross $\mathcal{X} = \{x \in \reals^2 : x_1^2 = x_2^2\}$ is not an embedded submanifold. It is not sufficient to show that $x \mapsto x_1^2 - x_2^2$ is not a local defining function at the origin. It is necessary to show that no local definition function exists at that point. Proceeding by contradiction, assume that $\mathcal{X}$ is a smooth manifold. Analyze the tangent space of the origin.

Suppose for contradiction that $\mathcal{X}$ is a manifold. Then, it must have a dimension: either 0, 1 or 2. The only way a submanifold of $\reals^2$ can have dimension 2 is if it is an open subset of $\reals^2$. Clearly, $\mathcal{X}$ is not open. Thus, if it is a manifold, its dimension must be 0 or 1. Let's derive a contradiction to this.

Define $\gamma_1 \colon \reals \to \mathcal{X}$ by $t \mapsto t \cdot v_1$ and $\gamma_2 \colon \reals \to \mathcal{X}$ by $t \mapsto t \cdot v_2$ with $v_1 = (1, 1)$ and $v_2 = (1, -1)$. These curves are clearly well defined and smooth thus by the definition of tangent space we have that $v_i = \gamma_i'(0)$ is in $\T_{(0,0)} \mathcal{X}$ for $i = 1, 2$.

But then we have that $\dim(\T_{(0,0)} \mathcal{X}) \geq 2$ (in fact, equals 2), which is in contradiction with $\dim(\T_{(0,0)} \mathcal{X}) = \dim(\mathcal{X}) \in \{0,1\}$.
