Show that the set $$\calM = \{(x, y) \in \Rd \times \Rd \colon x^{\top} x=1, y^{\top}y=1, x^{\top} y=0\}$$ is an embedded submanifold of $\calE = \Rd \times \Rd$. Define a function $h \colon \Rd \times \Rd \to \Rk$ for some $k$ such that $\calM = \{ (x, y) \in \Rd \times \Rd : h(x, y) = 0 \}$, and study the properties of $h$. Equivantly, you can also define $h$ over the matrices in $\reals^{d \times 2}$.

The direct approach is to let $h(x, y) = (x^\top x - 1, y^\top y - 1, x^\top y)$ from $\Rd \times \Rd \to \reals^3$, and to study the rank of the differential of $h$.

Alternatively, and in a way that more transparently suggests how to proceed to study a general Stiefel manifold, proceed as follows.

Letting the columns of $X \in \reals^{d \times 2}$ be $x$ and $y$ we can reformulate $\calM$ as, \begin{aligned} \calM = \{X \in \reals^{d \times 2} : X^\top X = I\}. \end{aligned} Let \begin{aligned} h \colon \reals^{d \times 2} \to \Sym(2), \quad \quad h(X) = X^\top X - I, \end{aligned} where $\Sym(2) := \{U \in \reals^{2 \times 2} \; | \; U=U^\top\}$. As $h$ is clearly smooth and $\calM:=h^{-1}(\{0\})$ we only need to show that $\rank \; \D h(X) = \dim(\Sym(2)) = 3$ for all $X \in \calM$. This is equivalent to show that $\D h(X)$ is surjective for all $X \in \calM$. By a straightforward computation we get that $\D h(X)[V] = X^\top V + V^\top X$ for $X \in \calM$ and $V \in \reals^{d \times 2}$. Thus for arbitrary $W \in \Sym(2)$ we have that \begin{aligned} \D h(X)\bigg[\frac{1}{2} X W\bigg] = \frac{1}{2} (X^\top X W + W^\top X^\top X) = \frac{1}{2}(W + W^\top) = W, \end{aligned} which shows that $\D h(X)$ is surjective and hence that $\calM$ is a manifold.

What are the tangent spaces of $\calM$? What is the dimension of $\calM$? Once the previous question is solved, you have all the ingredients you need.

Continue from above.

The tangent space to $\calM$ at $X \in \calM$ is given by \begin{aligned} \T_X \calM = \ker \D h(X) = \{V \in \reals^{d \times 2} : X^\top V + V^\top X = 0\}. \end{aligned}

By definition, the dimension of $\calM$ is the dimension of any of its tangent space,s hence we have (by the rank-nullity theorem applied to $\D h(X)$) $\dim \calM = \dim(\reals^{d \times 2}) - \dim(\Sym(2)) = 2d-3$.
