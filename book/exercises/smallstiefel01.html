Show that the set $$\calM = \{(x, y) \in \Rd \times \Rd \colon x^{\top} x=1, y^{\top}y=1, x^{\top} y=0\}$$ is an embedded submanifold of $\calE = \Rd \times \Rd$. What is the dimension of $\calM$ ? What are the tangent spaces of $\calM$ ? Reformulate the set $\calM$ as a matrix equation.

Letting the columns of $X \in \reals^{d \times 2}$ be $x$ and $y$ we can reformulate $\calM$ as, \begin{aligned} \calM = \{X \in \reals^{d \times 2} : X^\top X = I\}. \end{aligned} Let \begin{aligned} h \colon \reals^{d \times 2} \to \symm(2), \quad \quad h(X) = X^\top X - I, \end{aligned} where $\symm(2) := \{U \in \reals^{2 \times 2} \; | \; U=U^\top\}$. As $h$ is clearly smooth and $\calM:=h^{-1}(\{0\})$ we only need to show that $\rank \; \D h(X) = \dim(\symm(2)) = 3$ for all $X \in \calM$. This is equivalent to show that $\D h(X)$ is surjective for all $X \in \calM$. By a straightforward computation we get that $\D h(X)[V] = X^\top V + V^\top X$ for $X \in \calM$ and $V \in \reals^{d \times 2}$. Thus for $W \in \symm(2)$ we have that \begin{aligned} \D h(X)\bigg[\frac{1}{2} X W\bigg] = \frac{1}{2} (X^\top X W + W^\top X^\top X) = \frac{1}{2}(W + W^\top) = W, \end{aligned} which shows that $\D h(X)$ is surjective and that $\calM$ is a manifold.

By definition we have that the dimension of $\calM$ is given by $\dim(\reals^{d \times 2}) - \dim(\symm(2)) = 2d-3$.

Lastly, the tangent space of $X \in \calM$ is given by \begin{aligned} \T_x \calM = \ker \D h(X) = \{V \in \reals^{d \times 2} : X^\top V + V^\top X = 0\}. \end{aligned}
